Accomplishments

We have a fully working interactive smoke simulator running in real time on the GPU. All four core systems are implemented and integrated:

  • Fluid simulation — Jos Stam's Stable Fluids solver (advection, diffusion, pressure projection) runs at 90 fps on a 128×128 grid.
  • Letter mask — "CS184" is rasterized from 5×7 pixel-font glyphs scaled to grid cells. Smoke is continuously emitted from these cells, forming the letters.
  • Rendering — A fullscreen quad shader maps the density field to a blue-grey smoke color with a smooth tone curve on a dark background.
  • Mouse wind — Moving the mouse injects a velocity impulse into the fluid grid in a small radius around the cursor, visibly pushing the smoke. Buoyancy makes smoke rise naturally; a global decay rate fades displaced smoke so the letters gradually re-form.
  • GUI controls — Live sliders for emit rate, wind strength, buoyancy, and decay rate. Keyboard shortcuts: P pause, R reset, W toggle wind, ESC quit.

Preliminary Results

On launch, "CS184" appears as white smoke columns rising from letter-shaped emitters against a dark background. Moving the mouse through the letters scatters the smoke; once the cursor moves away, the letters gradually re-form as new smoke is emitted and residual displaced smoke decays. The effect is clearly readable as interactive smoke typography running at smooth, real-time frame rates.

Parameter values that produce strong results: emit rate 50, decay 0.01, buoyancy 0.5, viscosity 0. At these settings the letters remain legible while the smoke still drifts naturally upward.
